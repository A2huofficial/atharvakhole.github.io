EzLists

EzLists
App User Interface Design for a grocery shopping list app. This project was a part of a school project where I pitched a concept for an app, designed the user experience and interface and developed the app to the prototype phase.

Project Brief
Managing household supplies/groceries is a hassle in general. This is especially true for students living with roommates.
With roommates, most students buy their own groceries to avoid the hassle of splitting bills and keeping track of everyone else's supplies.

The Goal: 
The app sets out to make grocery shopping more efficient and time saving for users.
The app will make it so that every individual in the household does not have to go out to buy their own supplies. Users can use synchronized lists and add items to their lists on the go. The other roommates will be able to see these lists when they are out grocery shopping.
EzLists also comes with an inventory management system to keep track of supplies at home so that you never run out of groceries unexpectedly. 
Target Audience: 
Students / Young Adults (18-25)
The app’s main target users are students who live with roommates.

The Process
My process for this app involved three main phases.
Research

Ideation

Design
Research
For the research phase I started with a Case Study on another similar app, SplitWise.

Image taken from https://www.splitwise.com/

I then followed up the research and my findings with a problem analysis. Then I proceeded to identifying the user needs through a user persona.

Finally at the end of my research, I moved into the ideation phase.

Ideation
I began ideation with a basic userflow by developing user journeys.
I then made low fidelity wireframe sketches.
Design
I started the design process with a moodboard.
Following the moodboard, I produced the style guide, which would serve as a design guideline during the final mockups for the app.
Finally I produced a promotional video for the app for social media platforms.
